2023-02-09

Teen Pregnancy

Measure

About

This indicator reports the rate of pregnancies per 1,000 females aged 15 to 19 years old with residence in the relevant location.

  • Why is this important? According to VDH, teen pregnancy has unique medical risks as lack of prenatal care, high blood pressure, premature birth, low birth weight, STDs, and postpartum depression. Apart from health concerns, teen pregnancy also has serious social and economic consequences. Teen births are more likely to lead to poor outcomes for both teenage mothers and their children.
  • Further Considerations: ADD ME
  • Trend: Virginia, Albemarle and Charlottesville all show a steady decline in teen pregnancies. In the most recent period available, the teen pregnancy rate per 1,000 was 4 in Albemarle, 7.6 in Charlottesville, and 9.3 in Virginia. According to the CDC, the national teen pregnancy rate has declined continuously since 1991. Research suggests the declines nationally are a function of increased contraceptive availability and use as well as decreased sexual activity.
  • How do we measure this? The teenage pregnancy rate is the sum of live births, legal induced abortions, and natural fetal deaths per 1000 women aged 15-19 years as estimated by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) Bridged-Race Postcensal population estimates.
  • Data source: Source: Virginia Department of Health, Division of Health Statistics, Teenage pregnancies, live births by city/county. 2000-2022. https://apps.vdh.virginia.gov/HealthStats/stats.htm. VDH provides downloadable files from which we pull the locality and statewide counts.
